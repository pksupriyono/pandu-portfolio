Research interests

Open Development

Recent decades have seen the rise of the open movement, most tangibly in the forms of open data, open access and open source. In light of this, a new development paradigm has emerged: Open Development. Considering the promise of inclusion and participation within this premise, the extent to which inclusion can be effectively realized deserves particular attention. Who gets to decide the "meanings" of data? Whose ontologies are used? What does this mean for people on the ground, and development cooperation?

ICT adoption in non-profits

Developments in Web 3.0 can offer new ways in which non-profits make use of ICTs. Web 3.0 is characterized by its connective element: it is supposed to connect data, knowldge and people. Within this context it is useful to ask critical questions about the role that Web 3.0 can have in the non-profit sector based on the lessons that the past few decades have taught us. Previous developments in technologies have emphasized, disrupted or created new social dynamics, and to solely talk about technical infrastructures would only be scratching the surface.
