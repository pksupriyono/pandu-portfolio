Research interests

Open Development

Recent decades have seen the rise of the open movement, most tangibly in the forms of open data, open access and open source. Indeed, one of the new development paradigms in the Global South has been termed as being open as well. Considering the promise of inclusion and participation within this premise, the extent to which inclusion can be effectively realized deserves particular attention. Who gets to decide the "meanings" of data? Whose ontologies are used? What does this mean for people on the ground, and development cooperation?

The notion of open development is becoming increasingly relevant considering technological advancements within the information age, such as smart cities.

ICT adoption in non-profits

Developments in Web 3.0 can offer new ways in which non-profits make use of ICTs. Web 3.0 is characterized by its connective element: it is supposed to connect data, knowldge and people.
